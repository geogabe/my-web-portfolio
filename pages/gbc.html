Windows 8 App

Challenge

Great British Chefs wanted to bring their premium recipe content to the new Windows 8 platform — but the Metro design language required a completely different approach to layout, navigation, and content hierarchy than a traditional web or mobile experience. The challenge was to make the app feel native to Windows 8 while preserving the brand's editorial quality.

Process

As sole designer on the project, I worked directly with the Great British Chefs team and the client across the full UX and UI lifecycle — from information architecture and navigation models through to pixel-level visual design. I designed to the Windows 8 design guidelines while pushing the visual language to match the premium food editorial aesthetic the brand was known for.

Outcome

A Windows 8 app that earned Microsoft App of the Month recognition on launch — a testament to both the quality of the design and its native integration with the platform. The app brought Great British Chefs' recipe library and chef profiles to the Windows Store with a visual language that felt premium, editorial, and entirely at home on the platform.

01 — Final Designs

The app

The Great British Chefs Windows 8 app uses the Metro panoramic layout to create an immersive recipe browsing experience — full-bleed food photography, chef profiles surfaced alongside their signature dishes, and a step-by-step cooking mode designed for use in the kitchen while the app remains open on screen.
