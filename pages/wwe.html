WWE My Universe —
Windows 8 App

A dedicated WWE fan experience for Windows 8 — bringing superstar profiles, event schedules, and championship history to the platform in a bold, high-impact visual language.

Challenge

WWE's brand is loud, physical, and theatrical — built on larger-than-life personalities and high-energy production values. Translating that into a Windows 8 Metro app required finding a way to honour the brand's intensity while working within the clean, typographic constraints of Microsoft's design language. The challenge was to make the app feel unmistakably WWE without breaking the platform's conventions.

Process

As sole designer, I worked directly with the client to define the content architecture — superstar profiles, event listings, championship history, and news — and to build a visual system that could carry WWE's brand weight within the Metro grid. Photography was treated as the primary visual element, with bold cropping and high-contrast type to match the energy of the brand.

Outcome

A Windows 8 app that brought the WWE universe to the Microsoft Store — superstar rosters, event schedules, championship history, and news content all unified under a visual language that felt native to the platform while carrying the full weight of the WWE brand. A strong early showcase of what entertainment apps could look like on the Metro platform.

01 — Final Designs

The app

WWE My Universe uses the Metro panoramic layout to move through the roster, events, and championship history with full-bleed wrestler photography and bold, high-contrast type. The visual system needed to feel as dramatic as a live event while remaining scannable and navigable for fans exploring the app on their own.
