Challenge

Oil and gas transportation operations relied on disconnected reports and manual tracking — making real-time decisions about shipments, routes, and exceptions nearly impossible without significant lag. The brief was to prove, through a working POC, that a unified tracking interface could transform operational decision-making.

Process

Working in a rapid POC sprint, I sketched the core user journeys with logistics stakeholders, identifying the critical decision points where real-time data would have the most impact. Low-fidelity wireframes were validated quickly before moving into high-fidelity UI to demonstrate the full experience to stakeholders.

Outcome

A proof-of-concept supply chain dashboard demonstrating real-time shipment tracking, route optimisation recommendations, and exception alerts — presented as a fully designed end-to-end experience across web and mobile, giving stakeholders a tangible vision for the platform's potential.

01 — Process

Sketching the journey

The POC started on paper — mapping the operational flow from shipment dispatch through to delivery confirmation, identifying where decision-makers needed data and what actions they needed to take. These sketches formed the backbone of the design sprint before any screen was built.

02 — Final Designs

The proof of concept

The final POC delivered a fully designed tracking interface — live shipment map with route visualisation, shipment detail panels with optimisation scores, and an exception management workflow. Designed for both large operations displays and on-the-go mobile access.
