F1 Race Tracker —
Pit Crew Companion

A real-time race companion app giving F1 pit crews instant access to lap data, tyre strategy, and competitor intelligence during a live Grand Prix.

Challenge

F1 pit crews operate in one of the most data-dense, time-critical environments in sport. Existing tools were built for the garage wall — not for a handheld device in the pit lane. The challenge was to compress race-critical data into a mobile interface that could be read and acted on in seconds, under race conditions.

Process

I researched how pit crews consume race data — lap deltas, tyre degradation curves, competitor pit windows — and identified the most time-sensitive decisions the app needed to support. The interface was designed for glanceability first, with progressive disclosure for deeper analysis between stints.

Outcome

A focused pit crew companion app built around the three most critical data layers during a live race: lap times and gaps, tyre strategy visualisation, and competitor pit stop tracking. Designed for speed, legibility, and one-handed use under the noise and pressure of a Grand Prix weekend.

01 — Final Designs

The companion app

The F1 Race Tracker distils live race telemetry into a mobile-native interface — lap data at a glance, tyre windows visualised across the stint, and competitor activity surfaced the moment it happens. Every screen is designed to be readable in under two seconds, in bright sunlight, wearing gloves.
