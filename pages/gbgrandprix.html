Less Than a Second
in Formula 1

A data visualisation installation for the British Grand Prix — exploring the margins that separate winners from the field in the world's fastest motorsport.

Challenge

Formula 1 race data is extraordinarily dense — lap times measured in thousandths of seconds, tyre degradation curves, sector-level competitor splits. The challenge was to transform this raw telemetry into a compelling exhibition piece that could engage a general audience at the British Grand Prix while doing justice to the complexity underneath.

Process

Working in a team of three, I led the full visual treatment of the installation. A data analyst crunched the race dataset; a second designer handled the narrative structure; I owned the visual language, typography, colour system, and the final rendered compositions. The goal was something that felt as fast and precise as the sport itself.

Outcome

A data visualisation installation that made the marginal differences in F1 visible and emotionally legible — showing how less than a second across a race distance separates the front of the grid from the midfield. The visual system was built to work at large exhibition scale while remaining readable close up.

01 — Final Designs

The installation

The visual system translates F1 timing data into a spatial, sculptural form — lap gaps become arcs, sector times become colour fields, and the race narrative emerges from the data itself. Every typographic and colour decision was made to reinforce speed, precision, and the drama of motorsport competition.
